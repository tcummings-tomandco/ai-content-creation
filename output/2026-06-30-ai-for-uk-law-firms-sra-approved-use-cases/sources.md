# Sources: What can UK law firms use AI for in 2026?

Accessed: 2026-06-30

---

## 1. SRA Code of Conduct for Solicitors — Paragraphs 3.2 and 3.3

**URL:** https://www.sra.org.uk/solicitors/standards-regulations/code-of-conduct-solicitors/  
**Publisher:** Solicitors Regulation Authority  
**Claim supported:** Solicitors must ensure their service is competent and timely (3.2) and must maintain their competence and keep professional knowledge and skills up to date (3.3). These paragraphs are the primary regulatory basis for AI oversight obligations.

---

## 2. SRA Compliance Tips for Solicitors — AI and Technology (updated 9 February 2026)

**URL:** https://www.sra.org.uk/solicitors/resources/innovate/compliance-tips-for-solicitors/  
**Publisher:** Solicitors Regulation Authority  
**Claim supported:** COLP is responsible for AI governance; firms must make clear to clients where they are interfacing with AI; client confidentiality must be maintained; solicitors must understand AI tools well enough to review outputs critically; professional responsibility cannot be transferred to the AI tool.

---

## 3. SRA Risk Outlook Report: The Use of Artificial Intelligence in the Legal Market (November 2023)

**URL:** https://www.sra.org.uk/sra/research-publications/artificial-intelligence-legal-market/  
**Publisher:** Solicitors Regulation Authority  
**Claim supported:** Three-quarters of the largest solicitors' firms were using AI in November 2023, "nearly twice the number from just three years ago"; over 60% of large firms were exploring generative AI; approximately one-third of small firms were exploring AI. Used for Tom & Co Layer 2 calculation of ~12pp annual adoption velocity (2020-2023).

---

## 4. Ayinde v London Borough of Haringey and Al-Haroun v Qatar National Bank [2025] EWHC 1383 (Admin) — 6 June 2025

**URL:** https://www.fenwickelliott.com/research-insight/newsletters/dispatch/archive/ayinde-v-haringey-al-haroun-v-qatar-national-bank-qpsc-anor  
**Publisher:** Judgment reported by Fenwick Elliott; original High Court judgment EWHC 1383 (Admin)  
**Claim supported:** Judgment handed down 6 June 2025 by Dame Victoria Sharp P. In Al-Haroun, 18 of 45 cited cases did not exist. Court found "a language model such as ChatGPT is not capable of conducting reliable legal research." Wasted costs orders granted. Solicitors referred to SRA. Non-precedent-setting leniency; future breaches face "severe sanction."

---

## 5. SRA Press Release: SRA Approves First AI-Driven Law Firm — Garfield.Law (spring 2025)

**URL:** https://www.sra.org.uk/sra/news/press/garfield-ai-authorised/  
**Publisher:** Solicitors Regulation Authority  
**Claim supported:** Garfield.Law was the first purely AI-based firm authorised to provide regulated legal services in England and Wales. Focus: small business debt recovery through the small claims court for debts up to £10,000.

---

## 6. SRA Authorises LawFairy — Second AI-Powered Law Firm (February 2026)

**URL:** https://www.globallegalpost.com/news/sra-authorises-new-ai-powered-law-firm-lawfairy-590898458  
**Publisher:** The Global Legal Post  
**Claim supported:** LawFairy received SRA authorisation in February 2026. Focuses on immigration law (visa eligibility, sponsorship, citizenship routes). Uses deterministic AI (structured decision pathways) not generative AI. SRA stated this does not endorse AI as a replacement for lawyers in complex work.

---

## 7. Law Society: Generative AI — The Essentials

**URL:** https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/ai-and-lawtech/generative-ai-the-essentials  
**Publisher:** The Law Society  
**Claim supported:** Law Society guidance on AI essentials for solicitors; cited as a training resource recommendation. The Law Society's model client care letter now includes a placeholder for AI disclosure.

## 8. ICO: Guidance on AI and Data Protection

**URL:** https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/artificial-intelligence/guidance-on-ai-and-data-protection/  
**Publisher:** Information Commissioner's Office  
**Claim supported:** UK GDPR obligations apply to AI systems processing personal data; DPIAs required before deploying AI at scale with client data. Cited as the primary reference for law firms' data protection baseline obligations.

## Tom & Co Layer 2 Calculation (Recipe C)

**Stat:** AI adoption velocity among UK's largest solicitors' firms, 2020-2023  
**Value:** approximately 12 percentage points per year  
**Method:** SRA Risk Outlook (November 2023) states 75% of largest firms were using AI, "nearly twice the number from just three years ago." Implied baseline (November 2020): approximately 37.5%. Change 2020-2023: 37.5 percentage points over 3 years = 12.5pp per year, rounded to ~12pp for conservatism.  
**Cited in article as:** "Tom & Co analysis of the SRA's Risk Outlook Report (November 2023)"
