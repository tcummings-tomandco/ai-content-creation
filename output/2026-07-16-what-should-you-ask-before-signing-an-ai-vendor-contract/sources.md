## 1. ICO: Contracts and third parties (AI and data protection risk toolkit)

**URL:** https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/advice-and-services/audits/data-protection-audit-framework/toolkits/artificial-intelligence/contracts-and-third-parties/
**Publisher:** Information Commissioner's Office
**Claim supported:** Due diligence checks on an AI provider, and a data protection impact assessment, need to happen at procurement stage. Used in "When do you need an AI vendor contract checklist?" and to support the audit-rights and algorithmic impact assessment questions in "Liability, IP and audit rights".

## 2. ICO: What needs to be included in the contract?

**URL:** https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/accountability-and-governance/contracts-and-liabilities-between-controllers-and-processors-multi/what-needs-to-be-included-in-the-contract/
**Publisher:** Information Commissioner's Office
**Claim supported:** UK GDPR Article 28 requires a written contract specifying the subject matter, duration, nature and purpose of processing, type of personal data, categories of data subjects, controller instructions, confidentiality, sub-processor authorisation, deletion or return of data, and audit rights. Used in the "Does the contract include the Article 28 mandatory terms?" checklist item.

## 3. ICO: The Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 — what does it mean for organisations?

**URL:** https://ico.org.uk/about-the-ico/what-we-do/legislation-we-cover/data-use-and-access-act-2025/the-data-use-and-access-act-2025-what-does-it-mean-for-organisations/
**Publisher:** Information Commissioner's Office
**Claim supported:** Section 80 of the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 came into force on 5 February 2026, replacing UK GDPR Article 22 with a permission-based regime for automated decision-making, with mandatory safeguards (information, representations, human intervention) for significant decisions involving special category data. Used in the "What changes if the tool makes decisions about people?" checklist item and the answer paragraph.

## 4. Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU AI Act), Article 26

**URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj/eng
**Publisher:** Official Journal of the European Union
**Claim supported:** Deployers of high-risk AI systems must retain automatically generated logs for at least six months, assign human oversight, monitor operation and conduct impact assessments where required. High-risk deployer obligations under Articles 9–17 and 26 apply from 2 August 2026. Used in the "Does this count as a high-risk AI system?" checklist item, the comparison table, and key_stats.json.

## Sources considered and rejected

- Several third-party "AI vendor contract checklist" articles (law firm blogs, SaaS procurement blogs) surfaced useful framing (e.g. "ask whether inputs train the model outside your tenant") but were not cited inline, since they are agency/law-firm marketing content rather than primary sources with disclosed methodology. Where a point from this research proved sound, it is presented in this article's own voice rather than attributed to the blog.
- The Society for Computers and Law's sample AI contract clauses were noted as a useful practitioner resource but not cited, as SCL is a membership body publishing practitioner drafting notes rather than a regulator or named-methodology survey.
- A widely repeated claim that the EU AI Act's core high-risk compliance deadline has been provisionally pushed from 2 August 2026 to December 2027 could not be confirmed as formally adopted (Council adoption and Official Journal publication were still pending as of the research date). The article uses the technically-binding 2 August 2026 date and does not assert the delay as fact.
