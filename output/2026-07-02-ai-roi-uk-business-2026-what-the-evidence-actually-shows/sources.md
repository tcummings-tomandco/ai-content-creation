## 1. Powering Productivity: AI and the Future of UK Work (March 2026)

**URL:** https://www.britishchambers.org.uk/news/2026/03/half-of-smes-using-ai-with-limited-headcount-impact-so-far/
**PDF:** https://www.britishchambers.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Powering-Productivity-AI-and-the-Future-of-UK-Work_FINAL.pdf
**Publisher:** British Chambers of Commerce, in partnership with the University of Essex (Institute for Social and Economic Research) and Atos
**Claim supported:** 54% of UK firms were using AI by March 2026, up from 35% in 2025 and 23% in 2023, based on a survey of 668 UK businesses (94% SMEs). Also: net productivity expectation of +71pp for current AI users vs +46pp for planners, +26pp for the unsure, and -3pp for non-adopters over the next 12 months. Also: larger SMEs and B2B professional services firms lead adoption; smaller, consumer-facing and manufacturing firms lag.

## 2. ISER Essex: Major jump in tech adoption (19 March 2026)

## 5. McKinsey: The state of AI: How organizations are rewiring to capture value

**URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai
**Publisher:** McKinsey & Company (QuantumBlack), named research firm with disclosed survey methodology
**Claim supported:** 39% of organisations report any EBIT impact from AI; only 6% ("AI high performers") report a material impact of 5% or more. Workflow redesign is the strongest predictor of EBIT impact. Also source of the 10-20% cost reduction figure in software engineering, IT and manufacturing functions used as the input to the Tom & Co Layer 2 calculation below.

## 6. Bain & Company: Your AI Budget Is Growing. Your Returns Aren't. Here's Why. (1 June 2026)

**URL:** https://www.bain.com/insights/your-ai-budget-is-growing-your-returns-arent-heres-why/
**Publisher:** Bain & Company (Automation and AI Pathfinder Survey 2026, n=951 global companies with $100m+ revenue)
**Claim supported:** 37% of companies expected cost reductions of 10-20% from generative and agentic AI; 40% actually saw an improvement of 10% or less, illustrating the gap between AI budget growth and delivered return.
