## 1. EU AI Act — full text (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
**URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689  
**Publisher:** Official Journal of the European Union  
**Claims supported:**
- Article 2(1)(c) territorial scope: providers and deployers in third countries whose AI outputs are used in the EU are in scope
- Definitions of "provider" and "deployer" (Article 3)
- Prohibited AI practices (Article 5)
- AI literacy obligation (Article 4, in force 2 Feb 2025)
- Article 50 transparency obligations (in force 2 Aug 2026)
- Article 99 penalty structure: €35m/7%, €15m/3%, €7.5m/1%

## 3. EU AI Act Service Desk — Article 50: Transparency Obligations
**URL:** https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-50  
**Publisher:** European Commission  
**Claims supported:**
- Article 50 applies from 2 August 2026
- Chatbots and AI systems interacting with people must disclose AI nature to users
- AI-generated images, audio, and video must be labelled
- Fine: up to €15m or 3% global annual turnover

## Layer 2 calculation note

**Tom & Co analysis — EU AI Act fine break-even threshold (stat_002)**

Break-even turnover at which percentage-of-turnover cap equals the fixed euro cap:
- Tier 1 (prohibited AI): €35m ÷ 7% = €500m global annual turnover
- Tier 2 (high-risk/transparency): €15m ÷ 3% = €500m
- Tier 3 (misleading info): €7.5m ÷ 1% = €750m

EUR/GBP rate used: 0.845 (approximate market rate, June 2026)
€500m × 0.845 = £422.5m ≈ £420m (rounded conservatively)

Conclusion: for any UK business with global annual turnover below approximately £420m, the percentage-of-turnover cap is always the binding (lower) fine in Tiers 1 and 2. The fixed euro caps only bite for businesses with over £420m in annual turnover.

Source for penalty structure: EU AI Act Article 99 (EUR-Lex / EU AI Act Service Desk as above).
