## 1. Powering Productivity: AI and the Future of UK Work (March 2026)

**URL:** https://www.britishchambers.org.uk/news/2026/03/half-of-smes-using-ai-with-limited-headcount-impact-so-far/
**PDF:** https://www.britishchambers.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Powering-Productivity-AI-and-the-Future-of-UK-Work_FINAL.pdf
**Publisher:** British Chambers of Commerce, in partnership with the University of Essex (Institute for Social and Economic Research)
**Claim supported:** AI adoption reached 54% of UK firms by March 2026, up from 23% in 2023. 33% of SMEs in 2025 reported no plans to adopt AI at all, down from 43% in 2024. Among firms with no plans, 92% expect no productivity change from AI over the next year.

## 3. BridgeAI programme

**URL:** https://iuk-business-connect.org.uk/programme/bridgeai/
**Competition detail:** https://apply-for-innovation-funding.service.gov.uk/competition/2141/overview/0b4e5073-a63c-44ff-b4a7-84db8a92ff9f
**Publisher:** Innovate UK Business Connect / Innovate UK (UK Research and Innovation)
**Claim supported:** BridgeAI Innovation Exchange grants of £25,000-£50,000 per project, 100% funded with no match-funding requirement, open to UK-registered SMEs in priority sectors (agriculture, construction, creative industries, transport/logistics, and expanded Industrial Strategy sectors). Typical submission-to-contract timeline of three to five months for a competition round.

## 4. Made Smarter Adoption programme

**URL:** https://www.madesmarter.uk/adoption/
**Publisher:** Made Smarter (government-backed manufacturing technology adoption programme, delivered with the Department for Business and Trade)
**Claim supported:** CapEx grants of up to £20,000 with 50% match funding for SME manufacturers investing in technology including AI. Regional round deadlines vary; South East extended to March 2027.

## 5. Innovate UK Smart Grants funding guidance

**URL:** https://www.ukri.org/councils/innovate-uk/guidance-for-applicants/guidance-for-specific-funds/smart-innovation-funding-guidance/
