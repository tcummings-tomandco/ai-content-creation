# Sources: What are the best agentic AI use cases for mid-market firms?

Accessed: 2026-07-01

---

## 1. DSIT AI Adoption Research (survey February to May 2025)

**URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-adoption-research/ai-adoption-research
**Publisher:** Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (research by IFF Research and Technopolis Group)
**Claim supported:** Agentic AI was the least-adopted AI technology at 7% of AI-using UK firms, against 85% for natural language processing. By sector, agentic AI use rose to 12% in agriculture, mining, manufacturing and energy, and 10% in information and communication, versus 5% across all businesses. Barriers: 71% of businesses had not identified a clear use for AI; 60% cited limited AI skills; 48% lacked tools or platforms. Methodology: 3,500 CATI interviews between 12 February and 2 May 2025, commissioned by DSIT.

## 2. ONS Business Insights and Conditions Survey (bulletin 8 January 2026, data late December 2025)

**URL:** https://www.ons.gov.uk/businessindustryandtrade/business/businessservices/bulletins/businessinsightsandimpactontheukeconomy/8january2026
**Publisher:** Office for National Statistics
**Claim supported:** Approximately 25% of UK businesses reported using some form of AI in late December 2025; for businesses with 250 or more employees the proportion was higher at 44%. Around 15% of businesses reported planning to adopt AI within the next three months, the largest proportion since the question was introduced in late September 2023. Used as one input to the Tom & Co Layer 2 calculation (large-firm AI adoption base rate).

## 3. Gartner: Over 40% of Agentic AI Projects Will Be Cancelled by End of 2027 (25 June 2025)

**URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027
**Publisher:** Gartner, Inc.
**Claim supported:** Over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by the end of 2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value, and inadequate risk controls. "Agent washing" (rebranding existing products without substantial agentic capability) is widespread; Gartner estimates only around 130 of thousands of agentic AI vendors are genuine.

**URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-03-05-gartner-predicts-agentic-ai-will-autonomously-resolve-80-percent-of-common-customer-service-issues-without-human-intervention-by-20290

## 5. Salesforce Agentforce production data (2025)

**URL:** https://www.salesforce.com/agentforce/
**Publisher:** Salesforce
**Claim supported:** Salesforce reported its own Agentforce assistant handled more than 380,000 support conversations and resolved 84% of them without a human. Cited as a named-vendor real-deployment figure (not a projection). Note: this is a vendor self-report and is presented as such in the article.

## 6. McKinsey: The Economic Potential of Generative AI (June 2023)

**URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/the-economic-potential-of-generative-ai-the-next-productivity-frontier
**Publisher:** McKinsey & Company / McKinsey Global Institute
**Claim supported:** Generative AI could add the equivalent of $2.6 trillion to $4.4 trillion annually across 63 use cases in 16 business functions; about 75% of that value falls across four areas: customer operations, marketing and sales, software engineering, and R&D. Published June 2023.

## 7. Deloitte: State of AI in the Enterprise 2026

**URL:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/content/state-of-ai-in-the-enterprise.html
**Publisher:** Deloitte
**Claim supported:** Agentic AI has the highest expected impact in customer support, with supply chain management, R&D, knowledge management, and cybersecurity also high-potential. High-volume transactional tasks (customer service, data entry, claims processing) are where agents give the clearest cost-and-speed return. Many so-called agentic initiatives are automation use cases in disguise; value comes from redesigning workflows, not layering agents onto old ones.

**URL:** https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/artificial-intelligence/guidance-on-ai-and-data-protection/

## Tom & Co Layer 2 Calculation (Recipe C / cross-source derivation)

**Stat:** Implied share of UK firms with 250 or more staff actually running agentic AI, mid-2025
**Value:** approximately 3%
**Method:** ONS BICS (late December 2025) reports 44% of firms with 250+ employees use some form of AI. DSIT AI Adoption Research (survey February to May 2025) reports 7% of AI-using firms use agentic AI. Combining the two: 0.44 x 0.07 = 0.0308, i.e. about 3.1% of large/mid-market UK firms were running agentic AI in mid-2025. Neither source published this combined figure. Conservative interpretation given the two surveys use slightly different populations and dates.
**Cited in article as:** "Tom & Co analysis of ONS and DSIT data (mid-2025)"
