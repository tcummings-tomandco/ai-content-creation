# Creating a Personalised Shopping Journey: How Tom&Co Delivered a Scalable Outfit Builder for LeMieux ![Tom&Co Cropped 1 1](/images/blog/Tom&Co -Cropped 1-1.png) In the fast-moving world of e-commerce, personalisation is more than just a trend—it's an expectation. That's why premium equestrian brand LeMieux partnered with digital agency Tom&Co to create a unique, interactive outfit builder that lets customers style their equestrian gear intuitively and engagingly. Built on Adobe Commerce (Magento), this tool has not only transformed the online shopping experience for LeMieux's customers but also demonstrated how flexible technology and creative thinking can combine to keep a brand ahead of the curve. ## Overview LeMieux, a leading equestrian brand renowned for its vibrant "Colour Story" collections, sought to enhance its online shopping experience by introducing a personalised outfit builder. Partnering with Tom&Co, the goal was to develop an intuitive, scalable tool that would allow customers to visualise and customise outfits for both riders and horses, thereby deepening engagement and boosting conversions and user satisfaction. ## Objectives - Design a user-friendly outfit builder that enables seamless real-time customisation of equestrian apparel and accessories across both platforms. - Ensure seamless integration across various product lines, including rider wear, horse gear, toy ponies, and dolls. - Leverage Adobe Commerce (Magento)'s flexibility to support future expansions and maintain a competitive edge. ## Results - **Enhanced User Engagement:** The interactive nature of the outfit builder led to increased time spent on the site and higher user satisfaction. - **Boosted Conversions:** Customers were more confident in their purchases, resulting in a notable uptick in conversion rates. - **Scalable Solution:** The modular design allowed for seamless integration of new product lines, demonstrating the tool's adaptability and long-term viability. ![image](/assets/(L)-Selection.png) ## Technology Stack **Platform:** Adobe Commerce (Magento) **Features:** Real-time product visualisation, responsive design, multilingual support, modular architecture At Tom&Co, we work with ambitious brands looking to push boundaries and create deeper connections with their customers. For premium equestrian brand LeMieux, that meant finding new ways to bring personalisation to the forefront of their e-commerce experience. Together, we developed an interactive and intuitive outfit builder that allows customers to mix, match, and visualise products in real time, while also keeping accessibility in mind. The result? A scalable, flexible feature that not only enhances the customer journey but also reflects the quality and attention to detail that LeMieux is known for. ![image](/images/blog/imgi_177_GROUP_lifestyle_toydoll_chloe_maya_olivia_3.jpg) ![image](/images/blog/imgi_170_GROUP_lifestyle_youngriderbaselayer-lizzylegging-orlapullon_dusk-aqua-blossom_1.jpg) ## A Collaborative Approach to Design From the outset, the project involved close collaboration between our design, UX/UI, and development teams, as well as the in-house team at LeMieux. The goal was to reimagine how customers could interact with products online, moving beyond static traditional product images to a more unique and immersive, hands-on personal experience. We began by mapping out the typical user journey and identifying key decision points: what product combinations do riders most often consider? How important is colour matching to them and their horse breeds? What role does visualisation play in purchase confidence? These insights shaped the foundation of the outfit builder. Using Adobe Commerce (Magento), we developed a fully custom front-end experience that integrates seamlessly with LeMieux's product catalogue. Riders can now style their look from head to hoof, choosing saddle pads, fly hoods, boots, rider wear, and more—all with instant visual feedback. ## Designing the Ultimate Outfit Builder The journey began with a simple goal: to create an immersive digital experience where riders could mix and match their gear before purchasing. Tom&Co's design team started by understanding LeMieux's customer—style-conscious equestrians who care about how they and their horses look and feel. The UX phase focused on interactivity, simplicity, and a clear visual representation of the products. Once this was finalised, the transition from a low-fidelity prototype to elevated designs was straightforward, ultimately resulting in a product that both LeMieux and Tom&Co were happy with. From there, Tom&Co's developers leveraged Adobe Commerce (Magento)'s powerful framework to build a dynamic visualiser. Customers could choose from a wide range of colours and styles for saddle pads, fly hoods, boots, and rider apparel, all rendered in real-time on the screen. The modular design allowed for seamless customisation of everything from the horse's coat colour to the rider's base layers, creating a cohesive and finished look for both. ![image](/images/blog/petrol_matching_riding_outfit_2.jpg) ## Rolling Out Personalisation Across Product Lines The initial launch focused on the rider. From there, we expanded the functionality to include horse breeds and coat colours, allowing users to customise their gear based on how it would look on their horse. This evolution of the experience helped reinforce the emotional connection between rider and horse, central to the LeMieux brand. Recognising the broad appeal of LeMieux's product range, we extended the outfit builder to the toy pony collection. Here, children (and parents) could enjoy the same personalisation journey on a smaller scale. Most recently, the tool has been adapted again—this time for LeMieux's growing line of dolls, further demonstrating the scalability of the solution. Each stage of the rollout was supported by our agile delivery model, ensuring consistent UX across all touchpoints and allowing for quick iteration based on customer feedback. ## Implementation **Phase 1: Rider and Horse Customisation** The initial rollout focused on enabling customers to personalise outfits for themselves and their horses. Users could select from various colours and styles, viewing their choices on realistic 3d renders. This feature enhanced the shopping experience by allowing customers to see how different combinations would look before making a purchase. **Phase 2: Expansion to Toy Ponies** Recognising the popularity of LeMieux's toy pony collection, the outfit builder was adapted to allow customisation of these miniature models. Children and collectors could now personalise their toy ponies with matching accessories, mirroring the adult experience playfully and engagingly. **Phase 3: Integration with Dolls** The most recent development extended the outfit builder to LeMieux's doll collection. This adaptation maintained the tool's intuitive design while catering to a younger demographic, further broadening the brand's appeal and reinforcing its commitment to personalisation across all product lines. ![image](/assets/Outfit-Builder-Banner-1x1-(1).webp) ## Introducing LeMieux's Toy Riders Collection We're thrilled to share LeMieux's latest launch—the Toy Riders Collection—bringing a whole new level of fun to the equestrian play experience. Now, young riders can be the proud owners of Chloe, Olivia, and Maya, three beautifully designed toy riders ready to saddle up and ride alongside their favourite LeMieux ponies. But the fun doesn't stop there. LeMieux fans can mix and match outfits, create their own adventures, and explore the full collection for hours of imaginative play. Each rider is crafted with the same attention to detail and quality that LeMieux is known for—making this launch not just adorable, but truly exciting for pony-mad little ones (and their parents too). This playful expansion of the brand is a brilliant example of LeMieux's ongoing innovation—and their commitment to engaging the next generation of riders.
